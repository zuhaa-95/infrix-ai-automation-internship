A crime scene is a location where a crime has taken place.
Evidence at a crime scene includes physical, biological, and digital evidence.
Fingerprints are one of the most common types of evidence found at crime scenes.
Blood spatter patterns can reveal information about the nature of a crime.
Footprints and tire tracks can help identify suspects at a crime scene.
DNA evidence is the most reliable form of biological evidence.
Witnesses are people who saw or heard something related to the crime.
A suspect is a person believed to be involved in a crime.
Motive is the reason why a person commits a crime.
Alibi is evidence that a suspect was elsewhere when the crime occurred.
Weapons found at crime scenes are carefully analyzed for fingerprints and DNA.
Security camera footage is important digital evidence in modern investigations.
Time of death can be determined through body temperature and rigor mortis.
Poison can be detected through toxicology reports during autopsy.
Burglary involves breaking into a property to commit a crime.
Murder is the unlawful killing of another person.
Robbery involves taking property from a person by force or threat.
Arson is the deliberate setting of fire to property.
Fraud involves deception for financial gain.
A detective analyzes clues and evidence to solve crimes.
